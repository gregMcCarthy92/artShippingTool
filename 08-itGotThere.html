| Step 8-1 |
| Inspect the Damage |



Nice job! The only catch now is that you need to inspect teh packages, or if you are lucky, the gallery staff will. This is probably the gold standard. Who kew the governement had so many resources on this right?

The good news is that the linked document is probably about a thousand percent too in depth for your purposes. Generally just take photos of the artwork at each stage in unpacking, inspecting for damage and taking a photo of anything that seems odd. There is a really high likelyhood that even if your work was damaged, you'll never be able to get any money out of the shipper, but it is always best to cover your ass when it comes to things like this.

Once you have your mountain of photos, make sure you get them off your phone. Chances are you are somewhere fun, or at least different right now if you are doing this, and losing your only viable chance at a claim with the shipper because you were out drinking and messed up your phone, or accidentally deleted them to make room for photos of your dog is a pretty lame way to loose out. Obviously some kind of offsite server or fancy tech solution is best, but who has the time or know how to set that up right? Just email them to yourself for the time being or shove them in your google drive. You never clean that out anyway right? They'll probabbly last as long as your interest in the piece is question does... So you should be fine right?
